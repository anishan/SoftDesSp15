			<h2><a href="http://www.foxnews.com/category/science/climate">Climate</a></h2>
			<h1 itemprop="headline">New York set for massive climate march</h1>
			<div class="article-info">
			<div> 		
			 		<p itemprop="author" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">By <span itemprop="name"><a href="/archive/james-rogers" itemprop="url" rel="author"> James Rogers</a></span></p><time itemprop="datePublished" pubdate datetime="2014-09-19T15:39-04:00">Published September 19, 2014</time><div itemprop="sourceOrganization" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization"><a itemprop="name" href="http://www.foxnews.com/">FoxNews.com</a></div></div>
			</div>		
			
			<div class="social-count">
					<div>
		                <a class="fbk" href="#"><span>Facebook</span></a><span class="fbk">0</span>
		                <a class="twt" href="#"><span>Twitter</span></a><span class="twt">0</span>
		                <a class="lf-comment" href="#"><span>livefyre</span></a>
	                	<a class="email" href="#"><span>Email</span></a>
			            <a class="print" href="#"><span>Print</span></a>	                
		            </div>
	            </div>
            <div itemprop="articleBody">
			
			<div class="m">
						<img src="http://a57.foxnews.com/global.fncstatic.com/static/managed/876/493/ChimneysClimate.jpg?ve=1&tl=1" alt="ChimneysClimate.jpg" itemprop="image">
						<div class="caption">
							<p itemtype="http://schema.org/Organization" itemscope="" itemprop="sourceOrganization"><span itemprop="name" class="source">&nbsp;(REUTERS/Peter Andrews/Files)</span></p>
						</div>
					</div>
						
					<p>Organizers of the People’s Climate March expect more than 100,000 people to take part in the event in New York on Sunday Sept. 21.</p>

<p>Scheduled to take place two days before the city hosts the U.N. Climate Summit, the event is being touted as the largest climate march in history.</p><div class="mod-16"><div class="mod-ad-4 ad-container"><div class="ad gpt" id="tablet-300x250"></div><p class="advert-txt">ADVERTISEMENT</p></div><div class="mod-ad-4 ad-container"><div class="ad gpt" id="mobile-300x250"></div><p class="advert-txt">ADVERTISEMENT</p></div></div>

<p>“We’re expecting upwards of 100,000 people,” Hoda Baraka, a spokeswoman for 350.org., one of the march’s organizers, told FoxNews.com. Baraka noted that almost 100,000 people attended a similar march at the U.N. Climate Change Conference in Copenhagen in 2009.</p>

<p>U.N. Secretary General Ban Ki-Moon will take part in the New York march, as will billionaire environmentalist Tom Steyer.</p>

<p>“It’s not just mobilizing in New York – we have over 2,000 events scheduled worldwide,” said Baraka. “So many people are mobilizing, not just in New York, but all over the world – it’s bringing together people from all different walks of life, all of them united in a call for action for climate change.”</p>

<p>However, Dan Simmons, vice president for policy at the Institute for Energy Research, a Washington, D.C.-based free market think tank, told FoxNews.com that environmental campaigners are keen to avoid a repeat of the Copenhagen Summit, which was seen as <a target="_blank" href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2009/12/18/AR2009121800637.html">falling</a> short of its goals.</p>

<p>“Environmental activists are pulling out all the stops to try and get people to this march, because, I think, the president was embarrassed five years ago by the lack of public support for his position to limit greenhouse gas,” he said.</p>

<p>The Climate Summit on Sept. 23, which will be attended by President Obama, is timed to coincide with the start of a new U.N. General Assembly session. Advocates for a climate agreement are hoping that the event will breathe new life into the U.N.’s green agenda.</p>

<p>The U.N. has suffered a number of <a href="http://www.foxnews.com/world/2014/07/30/un-puts-new-momentum-behind-its-radical-green-agenda-for-climate-change/" target="_blank">blows</a> in its push for a climate agreement.&#160;Australia’s new government, for example, recently repealed its two-year old national carbon tax, and countries such as Canada, Russia, and Japan have refused to sign on for an extension to the Kyoto Protocol to combat greenhouse gases.</p>

<p>Earlier this week, the U.S. outlined its <a target="_blank" href="http://www.foxnews.com/science/2014/09/18/us-outlines-climate-plan-ahead-new-york-summit/">plan</a> for a possible global climate agreement.</p>
